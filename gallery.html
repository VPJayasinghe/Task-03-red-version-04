Sri Lanka in Pictures

Ella – Hill Country Escape

Ella, a charming town in Sri Lanka’s central highlands,is known for its stunning views, cool climate, and lush greenery. Surrounded by tea plantations, waterfalls, and misty mountains, Ella offers a perfect mix of adventure and relaxation.

Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya, nestled in the central highlands,is famed for its cool climate, colonial architecture, and picturesque landscapes. Often called “Little England,” this charming town is surrounded by rolling tea plantations, misty mountains, and lush gardens.

Horton Plains National Park

Horton Plains National Park, located in Sri Lanka’s central highlands, is a misty, windswept plateau known for its breathtaking natural beauty and ecological importance. The park’s most iconic feature is World’s End.

Anuradhapura

One of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, Anuradhapura is home to ancient stupas, royal palaces, and sacred Bodhi trees. It was the first capital of Sri Lanka and a center of Theravada Buddhism.

Polonnaruwa

This medieval capital features remarkably preserved ruins of palaces, temples, and statues. The Gal Vihara rock carvings are a highlight, showcasing the island’s finest stonework from the 12th century.

Kandy

Nestled in the hills, Kandy is Sri Lanka’s cultural capital. It's home to the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa) — one of the most revered Buddhist pilgrimage sites. The city also hosts the famous Esala Perahera, a grand cultural procession.

Mirissa – Beach Bliss & Whale Watching

Mirissa, a vibrant coastal town in southern Sri Lanka, is famous for its golden beaches, turquoise waters, and relaxed atmosphere. It's one of the best spots in the world for whale and dolphin watching.

Unawatuna – Coastal Charm & Coral Reefs

Unawatuna, located just south of Galle,is a popular beach destination known for its calm waters,coral reefs, and laid-back vibe. The golden sandy beach is ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and relaxing under palm trees.

Trincomalee – Serenity by the Sea

Trincomalee, on Sri Lanka’s northeastern coast, is known for its pristine beaches,crystal-clear waters, and rich cultural history.Nilaveli and Uppuveli beaches offer peaceful settings for swimming, snorkeling, and diving.

Yala National Park

Sri Lanka’s most famous wildlife park, Yala is known for its high density of leopards. Visitors can also spot elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles, and hundreds of bird species on a thrilling safari.

Udawalawe Safari

Ideal for elephant lovers, Udawalawe is one of the best places in Asia to see wild elephants up close. The park also offers views of buffalo, deer, jackals, and diverse birdlife.

Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage

Located along the Maha Oya River, this sanctuary cares for orphaned and injured elephants. Visitors can watch the gentle giants bathe, feed them, and learn about elephant conservation efforts.
